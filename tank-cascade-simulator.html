How it works

Every reservoir fills from rain at the top of the system. When you connect two tanks, water flows downhill automatically — but the rate is capped, the same way a bisokotuwa regulated outflow in ancient Sri Lankan reservoirs so pressure never spiked enough to burst the embankment.

Flow rate = maxOutflow × √(fill) — fuller tanks push harder, but never past the channel's limit. A full tank with no outlet simply spills, like a real spillway.
